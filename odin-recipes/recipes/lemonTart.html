Lemon Tart

Description

What makes this Lemon Tart so perfect? It’s the lemon curd filling.

It’s not too sweet but not mouth-puckeringly sour either, and so custardy it just melts in the mouth.

This is a classic French tart that’s elegant and pretty as a picture, yet the filling is as simple as can be: just eggs, sugar, butter and fresh lemon!

Ingredients

For the pastry

  • 500g plain flour
  • 1140g icing sugar
  • 250g unsalted butter
  • 4 egg yolks

For the filling

  • juice of 2 or 3 normal sized lemons
  • 140g caster sugar
  • 50ml double cream
  • 5 eggs

Step1

  1. To make the pastry, mix the flour and icing sugar in a bowl.
  2. Rub the butter into the flour with your fingers until crumbly
  3. Mix in the egg yolks. If the pastry is still too dry, add 1-2 tbsp water until it comes together.
  4. Roll into a ball and divide in half (freeze one half for another recipe).
  5. Flatten out the pastry with your hands, wrap the dough in cling film, then chill for at least 30 mins
  6. While the pastry is chilling, make the filling.
  7. Beat all the ingredients, except for the zest, together.
  8. Sieve the mixture, then stir in the zest.

Step2

  1. Roll out the pastry on a lightly floured surface to about the thickness of a £1 coin, then lift into a 23cm tart tin.
  2. Press down gently on the bottom and sides, then trim off any excess pastry.
  3. Stab a few holes in the bottom with a fork and put back in the fridge for 30 mins.
